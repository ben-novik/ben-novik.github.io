Working with kids

Why I work well with kids

I’ve spent years helping and taking care of my little brother and younger cousins, and I’ve worked with children at summer camp. Through all of that, I’ve learned how important patience is. Kids learn best when they feel comfortable, supported, and able to ask questions without pressure.

My approach to math

How it works

How sessions work

I offer one-on-one, in-person math tutoring and charge by the hour. The first session is mainly about understanding how your child learns and identifying any areas where they feel stuck.

We’ll look over homework, quizzes, and tests so I can see what’s confusing or intimidating. Every student learns differently, so I adjust my explanations and strategies to match what works best for them.

From there, we focus on building real understanding first. As confidence grows, grades and test scores naturally follow.

First meeting

What the first session looks like

Here’s what you can expect from our first hour together:

  • Quick introductions: I get to know your child, how they feel about math, and what’s been challenging.
  • Review of recent work: We’ll look at homework, quizzes, or tests to see where things started feeling unclear.
  • How they think: I’ll ask your child to talk through a few problems so I can see how they approach questions and where they get stuck.
  • Mini game plan: By the end of the session, I’ll have a clearer sense of what to focus on and how often it makes sense to meet.
  • Parent recap: I’ll share a quick summary with you of what I noticed and how I’d suggest moving forward.

The first session is relaxed, low-pressure, and built around making your child feel comfortable and heard.
